The Lumina Difference: Understanding Candle Waxes

Discover why the foundation of a candle matters for your health and home sanctuary.

Avoid
warning

Paraffin Wax

Derived from petroleum, paraffin is the most common and inexpensive wax used in mass-market candles.

When burned, it releases toxic volatile organic compounds (VOCs) and black soot, compromising indoor air quality.

Alternative
hive

Beeswax

A natural byproduct of honey production, beeswax is incredibly dense and burns very slowly.

While clean-burning and natural, it has a distinct inherent scent that can interfere with delicate fragrance profiles.

Lumina Choice
eco

Soya Wax

Crafted from the oil of soybeans, this renewable resource is biodegradable and entirely vegan.

It burns remarkably clean, produces zero petro-carbon soot, and offers an exceptional scent throw.

Why We Choose Soya

  • health_and_safety

    A Healthier Burn

    Unlike paraffin, soya wax does not release toxic carcinogens into the air you breathe. It ensures your sanctuary remains pristine.

  • water_drop

    Clean & Soluble

    Spills are easily cleaned with simple soap and warm water, leaving no stubborn, oily residue behind.

  • all_inclusive

    Longer Lasting

    Soya wax burns at a cooler temperature than paraffin, resulting in a candle that lasts significantly longer, extending your moments of calm.
